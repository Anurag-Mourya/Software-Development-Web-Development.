## Q1. Unit Digit

**The unit digit of (264)^102 + (264)^103 is:**

A) 0  
B) 4  
C) 6  
D) 8

**Answer: A) 0**

**5-Second Trick:** Unit digit of 4 cycles as 4, 6. Even power → 6, odd → 4. 6 + 4 = 10 → unit digit **0**.

**Explanation:** Unit digit of 4^n: if n is even → 6, if odd → 4. So 4^102 ends with 6, 4^103 ends with 4. Sum = 6 + 4 = 10. Unit digit = **0**.

## Q2. Divisibility by 11

**If 97215×6 is completely divisible by 11, the smallest whole number for x is:**

A) 3  
B) 2  
C) 1  
D) 5

**Answer: A) 3**

**5-Second Trick:** (Odd places sum) − (Even places sum) = multiple of 11. Odd: 9+2+5+6=22. Even: 7+1+x=8+x. 22−(8+x)=14−x=11 → **x=3**.

**Explanation:** For divisibility by 11: (Sum of digits in odd positions) − (Sum in even positions) = 0 or multiple of 11. Positions from right: 6(1), x(2), 5(3), 1(4), 2(5), 7(6), 9(7). Odd: 6+5+2+9=22. Even: x+1+7=8+x. 22−(8+x)=14−x. Set =11 → x=**3**.

## Q4. Remainder Theorem

**What is the remainder when 2^100 is divided by 7?**

A) 1  
B) 2  
C) 4  
D) 6

**Answer: B) 2**

**5-Second Trick:** 2^3 = 8 ≡ 1 (mod 7). So 2^99 = (2^3)^33 ≡ 1^33 = 1. Then 2^100 = 2^99 × 2 ≡ 1×2 = **2**.

**Explanation:** Remainder of powers of 2 when divided by 7 cycles: 2, 4, 1, 2, 4, 1... (cycle of 3). 100 mod 3 = 1. So remainder = first in cycle = **2**.

## Q13. HCF & LCM

**Three numbers have HCF 12 and LCM 240. Two numbers are 48 and 72. Find the third.**

A) 60  
B) 48  
C) 72  
D) 96

**Answer: A) 60**

**5-Second Trick:** For three numbers: HCF × LCM ≠ product. But LCM(48,72,x)=240 and HCF(48,72,x)=12. 48=12×4, 72=12×6, x=12×k where k co-prime with others. LCM of (4,6,k)×12=240 → LCM(4,6,k)=20. k=5 → x=12×5=**60**.

**Explanation:** Let numbers be 12a, 12b, 12c where a,b,c co-prime in pairs. Given 12a=48→a=4, 12b=72→b=6. LCM = 12×LCM(4,6,c) = 240 → LCM(4,6,c)=20. Since LCM(4,6)=12, we need LCM(12,c)=20. c=5 works. Third number = 12×5 = **60**.
